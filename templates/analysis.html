Original Document Text

EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT This Employment Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of January 15, 2024, by and between Acme Corporation, a Delaware corporation (the "Company"), and John Smith (the "Employee"). 1. POSITION AND DUTIES. The Company agrees to employ the Employee as Senior Software Engineer. The Employee shall report to the Chief Technology Officer and shall perform such duties as are customarily associated with such position. 2. COMPENSATION. 2.1 Base Salary. The Company shall pay the Employee a base salary at the rate of $120,000 per year, payable in accordance with the Company's standard payroll practices. 2.2 Bonus. The Employee shall be eligible for an annual performance bonus of up to 15% of base salary, based on achievement of objectives set by the Company. 2.3 Signing Bonus. The Company shall pay the Employee a one-time signing bonus of $5,000, payable within 30 days of the start date. 3. BENEFITS. The Employee shall be entitled to participate in all employee benefit plans generally available to Company employees, including health insurance, 401(k) plan, and paid time off. 4. TERM AND TERMINATION. 4.1 Term. This Agreement shall have an initial term of one year, commencing on January 15, 2024, and shall automatically renew for successive one-year periods unless either party provides notice of non-renewal at least 60 days prior to the end of the then-current term. 4.2 Termination. The Company may terminate this Agreement at any time with or without cause. The Employee may terminate this Agreement upon 30 days written notice. 5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY. All inventions, discoveries, developments, and other works conceived or made by the Employee during employment shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company. 6. NON-COMPETE. For a period of six months following termination of employment, the Employee shall not, within a 50-mile radius of the Company's offices, engage in any business that competes with the Company. 7. ARBITRATION. Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved by binding arbitration in accordance with the rules of the American Arbitration Association. 8. GOVERNING LAW. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware. IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have executed this Agreement as of the date first written above. COMPANY: By: ________________________ Name: Sarah Johnson Title: HR Director EMPLOYEE: ________________________ John Smith
